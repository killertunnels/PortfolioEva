This group academic project is framed within the report 2050 Scenarios: Four Plausible Futures, which analyzes potential future ways of living by addressing the connection between planetary health and social conditions. The proposed scenarios range from societal collapse to a model of full coexistence with nature.

SABOTAJE is a board game inspired by a vision of the year 2050 scenarios, where players attempt to either save or sabotage the environmental future. It is based on the values of the New European Bauhaus (NEB), which promotes three fundamental principles:
From the color palette to the character illustrations, SABOTAJE emphasizes aesthetics to provide a playing experience that is not only fun but also visually pleasing and stimulating.
From valuing diversity and equality for all to ensuring accessibility and affordability through audio and video instructions and tutorials, with easy-to-handle pieces and educational content on sustainability for the whole family.
SABOTAJE is made with recycled, renewable, and biodegradable materials, designed for easy component identification and featuring a modular design that facilitates disassembly and material separation.
In SABOTAJE, each player assumes a role: saboteur or savior. The saboteurs must ensure the mission's failure, while the saviors strive to complete the missions and eliminate the saboteurs. Their lives depend on the tower, symbolizing the planet's balance.
Happy Milk arises from the initiative to create a brand whose values are ethics, sustainability, and positivity. It offers a line of pocket-sized milk cartons in three different flavors, perfect for taking to school, work, or on trips.

This academic project was developed with the goal of creating a sustainable milk brand that directly addresses the sustainability challenges associated with milk commercialization. Additionally, the brand incorporates an anti-bullying message to promote positive social impact.
Considering the design challenge I was facing, I clearly saw the need to focus on animal exploitation in the dairy industry and the pollution that packaging could cause.

On the other hand, since the majority of people are part of the group of witnesses to a harassment situation, I found it crucial to address them, encouraging them to take action against any repeated abuse they witness.
